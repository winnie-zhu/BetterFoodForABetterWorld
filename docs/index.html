Better Food For A Better World

“Food deserts” are a major problem in the US today. This issue most profoundly affects impoverished neighborhoods. Food deserts are areas where people don’t have access to affordable healthy foods. Rather than offering nutritious foods, these neighborhoods usually have convenience stores and fast food restaurants, lacking proper and organic foods. In the US, 23.5 million people live in food deserts, and almost half of them are low-income.

In addition to food deserts, food waste is also an ongoing issue in our world. Food is being wasted everyday: on farms when crops aren’t “pretty enough” for the market, by retailers and consumers when they are past the “best-before” date, and in the kitchen when left unused and uneaten.
